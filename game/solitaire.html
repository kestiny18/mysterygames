Solitaire is a fun card game where you sort all 52 cards into four piles, one for each suit, from Ace up to King. Every time you clear a card, it feels like a small victory, just like Emma felt when she finished her break. If you're a newbie, it's okay. We explained everything you must know as simply as possible. Keep reading to learn the rules.

Controls

  • Click the stock pile (top left) to flip a new card.
  • Click or drag cards to move them between piles.
  • Double-click a card to send it to its foundation pile if it fits.

Rules

  • Make four foundations, one per suit (♥♦♣♠), stacking Aces first, then 2, 3, and so on up to King.
  • On the tableau (the seven columns in the center), build downward in numbers and alternate colors (red on black, black on red).
  • Only a King (or a stack starting with a King) can go into an empty column slot.
  • Use the stock (face-down deck) to draw new cards when you can't move on the tableau.
  • Win by moving every card into the four foundation piles.

How to Play Solitaire

  • Look at the cards you can see on the table and in the flipped pile.
  • Move cards by clicking or dragging: you can only put a red card on a black card or a black on a red, and the numbers must go down by one (for example, put a red 5 on a black 6).
  • If you uncover an Ace, it'll be automatically moved to one of the four empty spots at the top.
  • When you flip a card from the face-down deck, look if it fits on the table or on the foundations. If it fits, click or drag it there.
  • If you clear a whole column, only a King (or a King-led stack) can go into that empty spot.
  • Keep doing this: flip cards, move cards, and sort them into the top piles.
  • You win when all cards end up in the four piles, from Ace up to King. If you get stuck, click on the undo button to try a new approach or click on the new game to start fresh.

What is Solitaire?

Solitaire is a one player card game. You start with a shuffled deck and move cards between piles following simple rules. Your goal is to build four piles from Ace up to King.

How do I play Solitaire on OnlineGames.io?

Click "New Game" to shuffle and lay out seven tableau piles. Drag a face-up card (or a group in sequence) onto another tableau pile if it alternates color and steps down in rank (for example, red 6 on black 7). Click Aces to move them to the top (or use auto collect), then add 2, 3, 4 up to King in each suit. Click the stock pile to reveal new cards one by one. Keep going until all cards are in the foundation piles or no more moves are possible.

How do I win at Solitaire?

You win by moving every card into the four foundation piles (one per suit), each stacked from Ace through King. A game ends when no cards remain on the tableau, stock, or waste.

Is Solitaire a game of skill or luck?

Solitaire combines both.It takes skills as deciding which moves to make, when to draw from the stock, and how to uncover hidden cards. But it also takes some luck as the initial shuffle affects how many cards are accessible early on. If one of your aces are stuck deep in the tableu, it's hard to win such a round. But you can improve solitaire skills to overcome tough shuffles.

How Can I win more often in Solitaire ?

First try to find the aces and twos. Then move cards that uncover face-down cards first. Try not to empty a pile if you don't have a King ready to place. Use the undo button and try different moves and backtrack if you get stuck.

What does Draw 1 and Draw 3 mean?

  • Draw 1: Clicking the stock deals one card to the waste at a time.
  • Draw 3: Clicking the stock deals three cards at once in a small stack. Only the top card is playable until you draw again.

Draw 1 is easier; Draw 3 is more challenging. The Solitaire version on this page uses Draw 1 approach, so you can clear cards easier.

Can I undo a move?

Yes. Click the Undo button at any time to reverse your last move. Click on redo move it again.
