About This Game

Ever stayed late at school or walked through a dark hallway alone and felt like something was watching you? Did you just hear the footsteps? Is it the wind or someone whistling? Now imagine that same creep, but at an abandoned mental hospital where animatronic Poppy from FNAF stays in. That's exactly where Five Nights at Poppy throws you.

This creepy online horror game puts you in the shoes of a night shift security guard just as in first three FNAF games. But you're not watching out the Freddy's Pizzeria this time. Instead, you're patrolling the mental hospital Poppy is kept. She is angrier than ever. She's got wide glowing eyes, an eerie smile, and definitely she's not here to play.

With the lights flickering and cameras going out, your job is to survive five long nights from 12:00 AM to 6:00 AM. It starts out quiet. Maybe even boring. But by the time the static hits your screens and Poppy's shadow shows up at your door, it's a whole different game. Do whatever you can before you get jumpscared!

Controls

  • Left Click: Interact with lights, doors, and cameras
  • Click and Hold: Repair damaged camera feeds

How to Play Five Nights at Poppy

You begin each night sitting in a small security office with monitors all around. Each screen shows different parts of the hospital—like patient rooms, hallways, and a morgue. Your main jobis to watch out for Poppy.

Poppy moves slowly at first and switches back and forth between rooms. But as the nights go by, she becomes faster, sneakier, and more aggressive. She's not just creepy but also smart. If your cameras stay broken too long or you waste power-flipping switches, she'll strike. Here's what to keep track of:

  • Cameras: If one goes fuzzy or shows static, press and hold for a while to repair it.
  • Power: You have limited energy. Leaving doors closed or lights on drains it fast.
  • Corridors: Watch the left and right doors near your office. If you see glowing eyes, close the door fast!

Each night gets harder. You'll need to react quicker as the game goes on. It's a game of multitasking. One mistake, and Poppy's in your face. And trust us, the jumpscares are no joke.
